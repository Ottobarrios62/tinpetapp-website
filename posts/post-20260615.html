Best Pets for Apartment Living:
A Guide for Urban Pet Owners

June 15, 2026 · 6 min read

Living in an apartment doesn't mean you have to live without a furry (or feathery) companion. Millions of people successfully share small spaces with wonderful pets every day. The key is choosing the right animal for your lifestyle, your building, and your square footage — and then setting them up to thrive. Here's everything you need to know before you bring a new pet home to your city apartment.

What to Consider Before Getting an Apartment Pet

Before you fall in love with a breed or species, take an honest look at your situation. Ask yourself these questions:

  • Building rules: Does your lease allow pets? Are there weight or breed restrictions? Are there extra deposits or monthly fees?
  • Your schedule: How many hours a day are you away from home? Some pets, like dogs, need midday walks or company. Others, like cats or fish, handle alone time much better.
  • Noise tolerance: Thin walls mean neighbors will hear a vocal dog or a loud bird. Consider how much sound your pet produces.
  • Exercise access: Is there a dog park or green space within walking distance? If not, high-energy dogs will struggle in a small space.
  • Allergies: If you or anyone in your household is sensitive, opt for low-shedding or hypoallergenic breeds.

Matching a pet to your real lifestyle — not the lifestyle you wish you had — is the single most important decision you'll make. A pet that fits your life will be happy; one that doesn't will be stressed, and so will you.

Best Dog Breeds for Apartments

Contrary to popular belief, size alone doesn't determine whether a dog is apartment-friendly. Energy level, barking tendency, and temperament matter far more. A calm Great Dane can do better in an apartment than a hyperactive Jack Russell. That said, smaller and medium breeds do have a practical advantage in tight spaces.

Top apartment-friendly dog breeds include:

  • French Bulldog: Low energy, quiet, and sociable. Thrives indoors and loves short daily walks. One of the most popular city dogs for a reason.
  • Cavalier King Charles Spaniel: Gentle, adaptable, and happy with moderate exercise. Perfect lap dog that gets along with everyone.
  • Shih Tzu: Bred to be an indoor companion, the Shih Tzu is calm, affectionate, and doesn't need a lot of outdoor space to be happy.
  • Greyhound: Surprisingly well-suited for apartments — they're sprinters, not marathon runners, and spend most of the day lounging quietly.
  • Bichon Frisé: Hypoallergenic, low-shedding, and friendly. Adapts easily to apartment living and rarely barks excessively.

Whatever breed you choose, commit to two daily walks and regular socialization. An under-stimulated dog is a destructive dog — no matter the apartment size.

Best Cats for Apartment Living

Cats are naturally well-suited for apartment life. They're independent, clean, quiet (mostly), and don't need outdoor access to be healthy and happy — as long as you provide enough enrichment indoors. A tall cat tree, window perches, and interactive toys go a long way.

Breeds that adapt especially well to small spaces:

  • Ragdoll: Calm, affectionate, and low-energy. These gentle giants are happy to follow you around the apartment and curl up on the couch.
  • British Shorthair: Easygoing and quiet. They're content to entertain themselves and don't demand constant attention.
  • Persian: Laid-back and sweet. Persians prefer the comfort of indoors over the chaos of the outside world.
  • Scottish Fold: Adaptable and playful without being hyperactive. Great with families and other pets.

Even the most active cat breeds — like Bengals or Abyssinians — can live happily in apartments if you dedicate time each day to play. Rotate toys, use puzzle feeders, and consider adopting a second cat as a companion for when you're away.

Other Great Apartment Pets

Dogs and cats get all the glory, but other animals can make fantastic apartment companions:

  • Rabbits: Quiet, litter-trainable, and more social than you'd expect. They need space to roam for a few hours each day but thrive in indoor environments.
  • Guinea pigs: Gentle and social, they love interaction and rarely cause noise complaints. Keep them in pairs so they have company.
  • Budgies and cockatiels: Small parrots that bond closely with their owners. They do vocalize, so check your noise policy, but they're far quieter than larger parrots.
  • Fish: The ultimate apartment pet for busy schedules. A well-maintained aquarium is relaxing, beautiful, and completely neighbor-friendly.
